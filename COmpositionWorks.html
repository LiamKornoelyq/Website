Brass quintets

Coruscating Sky (2023)

d: 9:00

Few things in this world are more beautiful than the coruscate colors of the setting sun. Listen as the sun slowly sets over the horizon and night time falls upon the earth.
This piece was originally written to be played around the audience in a 360° area at the CCM View from the Edge concert. Unfortunately, due to recording equipment limitations this piece was only recorded in mono.

Performance by: Mason Kibble (Trumpet 1), Annaleigh Babcock (Trumpet 2), Corbin Russ (French horn\Mellophone), Madison Smith (Euphonium), Beatrice Aldrich (Tuba)

Systematic Haywire(2022)

d: 5:30

"Systematic Haywire" is a compelling musical exploration that delves into the intricate process of transitioning from order to chaos. This piece embarks on a sonic journey that mirrors the systematic breakdown of structure, leading to a state of controlled chaos. Through a dynamic interplay of musical elements, the composition challenges traditional notions of order and invites listeners to experience the beauty within entropy.

Methane Rain(2023)

d: 4:00

"Methane Rain" transports listeners to the enigmatic and otherworldly landscape of Titan, Saturn's largest moon. This piece is an imaginative sonic exploration of the atmospheric phenomenon unique to Titan – methane rain. As the music unfolds, it captures the ethereal beauty and mysterious allure of the celestial rains that cascade from Titan's hazy skies.

Winters First Snow(2022)

d: 6:00

"Winter's First Snow" is an evocative electronic composition that captures the serene and enchanting atmosphere of the first snowfall in a deep forest. This piece transports listeners to a magical realm where the delicate dance of snowflakes transforms the woodland into a pristine, otherworldly landscape.
